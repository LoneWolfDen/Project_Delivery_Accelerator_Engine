Call Notes - Discovery Session with FinServ Client
Date: 2024-04-02
Attendees: Mark Thompson (CTO), Lisa Wang (Head of Engineering), David Park (Consultant)

Context
Initial discovery call to understand their modernisation goals and constraints.

Current State
- Running 15 microservices on Kubernetes (EKS)
- 3 legacy monoliths still on EC2 (Java 8, .NET Framework 4.6)
- Monthly AWS spend approximately $180K
- Team of 12 engineers, 2 dedicated to platform/infra
- Using Jenkins for CI/CD, considering move to GitHub Actions

Pain Points
- Deployment frequency limited to bi-weekly due to manual testing
- No automated rollback capability
- Observability gaps: logs in CloudWatch but no distributed tracing
- Secret management inconsistent across services
- Database migrations are manual and error-prone

Goals
- Achieve daily deployments within 6 months
- Reduce incident response time from 45min to under 15min
- Decompose the 3 remaining monoliths
- Implement proper GitOps workflow
- Reduce cloud spend by 20% through right-sizing

Constraints
- Cannot disrupt production during business hours (AU market: 9am-5pm AEST)
- PCI-DSS compliance required for payment processing services
- Board has approved $500K budget for modernisation (12 month horizon)
- Must maintain SOC2 certification throughout transformation

Risks Identified
- Key person dependency: only 1 engineer understands the payment monolith
- No comprehensive test suite for legacy applications
- Vendor lock-in concerns with proprietary message queue

Next Steps
- David to prepare initial assessment report by April 10
- Mark to share architecture diagrams and runbooks
- Lisa to provide access to monitoring dashboards
- Follow-up call scheduled April 9 to review preliminary findings
